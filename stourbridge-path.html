STOURBRIDGE CANAL

FACTS AND HISTORY ABOUT STOURBRIDGE CANAL

Stourbridge Canal is roughly 5.8 miles in length. You can find a total of 16 locks around the path that starts from the Delph Locks in Brierley Hill and ends at Stourbridge Town. An Act was placed in 1662 to make the River Stour navigable from Stourton to Stourbridge. Originally built three years later, It was ruined due to floods.

Many years later, they made a proposal to join the canal with the Dudley No.1 Path and the Storuton Junction in order to link up with the Birmingham Canals. It was completed in 1779 which helped bring coal and other supplies to a world leading glass industry thriving in Stourbridge during the times. More about the History can be found at the Glass Cone Museum which is an attraction on the walk.
